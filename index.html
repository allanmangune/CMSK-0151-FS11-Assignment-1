Ben Hogan

Born on August 13, 1912

Ben Hogan, an American professional golf player, is considered one of the best golf ball strikers of all time. Hogan believed that mastering a golf swing required a lot of practice and repetition. Many believe that Hogan's golf swing is pure and have copied such a swing (n.d., 2006).

Hogan's accomplishments include the following:

  • World Golf Hall of Famer
  • Won nine major championships
  • PGA Tour leading money winner
  • PGA player of the year
  • Vardon trophy
  • Associated Press athlete of the year

Hogan wrote a book titled Five Lessons: The Modern Fundamentals of Golf. He says golfers should focus more on short games to create a repeatable golf swing. He says that a good golf grip significantly contributes to a golfer's success.

Following a successful career in golf, Hogan formed his golf company in 1953 in Fort Worth. After 11 years, he sold his company but kept the chairmanship position.
